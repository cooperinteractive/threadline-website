02 / Relationship to THREADLINE
Relationship to THREADLINE
PullSheet is a companion to THREADLINE, sharing the same database and design system. While both serve wardrobe professionals, each has a distinct role in the workflow.
THREADLINE
The smart organizer
Inventory management, budget tracking, returns logistics, AI-powered data entry. The operational backbone of a wardrobe department.
PULLSHEET
The visual storyteller
Lookboard builder, background removal, outfit compositions, PDF lookbook export. The creative tool for fittings and director review.
Connection Language
→
"Powered by your THREADLINE inventory"Use when describing data source for lookboards
→
"Built on THREADLINE data"Use when describing the integration in technical contexts
→
"Companion to THREADLINE"Use when positioning PullSheet alongside THREADLINE in marketing
→
"From THREADLINE to lookboard in one tap"Use when emphasizing the seamless workflow between apps
Shared Design DNA

Both apps share the same color palette, typography system, and interaction patterns. A user moving between THREADLINE and PullSheet should feel continuity, not context-switching. The only difference is the wordmark and the content domain.

Photography Rules
Do
  • . Clean, well-lit garment cutouts
  • . Flat-lay compositions with breathing room
  • . Real wardrobe racks and trucks (for atmosphere)
  • . Warm tones, natural light, editorial grading
  • . Grayscale for ghosted background imagery
  • . Focus on the clothes, not the people
Do Not
  • . Generic stock photography
  • . Lifestyle influencer aesthetics
  • . Over-saturated or neon-lit imagery
  • . Stark white e-commerce backgrounds
  • . Faces or identifiable people (privacy)
  • . Cluttered, messy rack shots as hero imagery
06 / Brand Voice
Brand Voice
PullSheet speaks like the creative lead in the wardrobe department. Professional but not corporate. Visual, confident, minimal. We show, we don't explain.
Voice Attributes
Visual
Not verbose
Confident
Not arrogant
Minimal
Not cold
Craft-aware
Not jargon-heavy
Do
"Build your lookboard in seconds"

Direct, active, time-aware. Tells the user exactly what they'll get and how fast.

Do Not
"Our AI-powered solution leverages..."

Corporate jargon. Nobody on set talks like this. If the tech works, let the results speak.

Do
"One tap. Clean cutout."

Minimal, punchy, action-oriented. Respects the user's time. The tool works. That is the point.

Do Not
"PullSheet is a revolutionary tool..."

Hyperbolic. If you have to say it's revolutionary, it probably isn't. Let the work prove it.

Do
"Powered by your THREADLINE inventory"

Connected, clear, factual. Shows the relationship without over-explaining the architecture.

Do Not
"Wardrobe PA-focused content management..."

Wrong terminology ("wardrobe PA" is not how this audience self-identifies), wrong framing ("content management" is IT-speak).

07 / Design Rules
Design Rules
PullSheet follows a light-mode-first approach: "Nordstrom meets Linear." Generous whitespace, subtle shadows, restrained accent usage. Every design decision should feel intentional and quiet.
+
Light-mode-first design Cream backgrounds, warm neutrals, natural light aesthetic. Dark mode is secondary.
+
Generous whitespace Let content breathe. Padding is a feature, not wasted space.
+
Subtle shadows, no heavy borders box-shadow: 0 2px 12px rgba(26,23,20,0.06). Borders at rgba(26,23,20,0.06).
+
Brown-gold accent is surgical Only primary CTAs and key highlights. Never applied broadly or as background fills.
+
Cards: 12px border-radius, 1px borders border: 1px solid rgba(26,23,20,0.06). Consistent across all card components.
+
Consistent type hierarchy Serif for headlines, sans-serif for body, mono for data. Never mix roles.
x
No gradients on UI elements Gradients are reserved for marketing hero sections and cover treatments only.
x
No neon, no glassmorphism No saturated accent colors. No frosted glass cards. No trendy effects.
