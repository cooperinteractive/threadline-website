"Which pocket was the handkerchief in?" It's 6 AM. You're standing on set. The scene you're shooting today was last filmed four weeks ago. And the continuity note you need is on a phone that no longer exists because the day player PA who took the photo finished their contract three weeks ago. This is not a rare occurrence. This is Tuesday. Continuity in wardrobe is brutal because the details are so specific. Which button was undone. Where the scarf was tucked. Which ring was on which finger. Whether the sleeves were rolled once or twice. When these photos and notes live in random camera rolls across multiple people's phones, the system is one personnel change away from collapse. Threadline keeps all continuity photos and notes in one place, organized by character, scene, and shooting day. Everyone on the department has access. Nobody's personal phone is the single point of failure. Because "I think it was the left pocket" isn't good enough when the director is waiting.
