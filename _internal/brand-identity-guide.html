04
Photography Direction
ThreadLine photography is warm, tactile, and atmospheric. Think: the golden hour of a fitting room. Garments are heroes — shot with depth of field, natural light, and rich texture. Avoid cold studio lighting, stark white backgrounds, or stock photography aesthetics.
Garment Details — Close, Textural
Rack Shots — Depth, Atmosphere
Workspace — Warm, Professional
Photography Rules
✓ Do
  • · Natural light, warm tones, golden hour
  • · Shallow depth of field on garment details
  • · Textured surfaces (wood, linen, leather)
  • · Real wardrobe trucks and fitting rooms
  • · Desaturated, editorial color grading
  • · Grayscale for secondary/background imagery
✗ Don't
  • · Cold fluorescent studio lighting
  • · Stark white backgrounds
  • · Generic stock photography
  • · Over-saturated colors
  • · Lifestyle influencer aesthetics
  • · Faces or identifiable people (privacy)
05
Brand Voice
ThreadLine speaks like the most competent person on set — direct, precise, industry-aware, never condescending. We respect the craft and the people who do it. Intelligence is invisible; it just works.
✓ ThreadLine Sounds Like
"3 items due back to Zara by Friday."

Specific, actionable, data-first. No filler, no emotion — just what you need to know to do your job.

✗ ThreadLine Never Sounds Like
"Hey there! 🎉 Your AI-Powered Smart Wardrobe Solution is ready!!"

No emojis in product. No exclamation marks. No "powered by AI" badges. No buzzwords. If you have to tell people it's smart, it isn't.

✓ Marketing Copy
"The wardrobe truck just went digital."

Confident, warm, industry-insider language. Speaks to someone who knows what a wardrobe truck is. Doesn't explain the obvious.

✓ Feature Description
"Scan it. Done."

Two words. Respects the user's time. The tool works — that's the whole point. Don't oversell what should be effortless.

Website Direction
